Gandhi’s mother, Putlibai, was completely absorbed in religion,did not care much for finery or jewelry,
divided her time between her home and the temple,
fasted frequently,and wore herself out in days and nights of nursing
whenever there was sickness in the family.2.html

Mohandas grew up in a home steeped in Vaishnavism—worship of the Hindu god Vishnu—with a strong tinge of Jainism,
a morally rigorous Indian religion whose chief tenets are nonviolence and the belief that everything in the universe is eternal.

Thus, he took for granted ahimsa (noninjury to all living beings), vegetarianism,
fasting for self-purification, and mutual tolerance between adherents of various creeds and sects
